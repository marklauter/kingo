The body of namespace definitions authored or administered together — an authoring and ownership word, not a stored thing; the stored, versioned unit is the [[namespace]].

## Examples

- A definition document declares two namespaces; the schema is what that document declares, the way a database schema is what DDL declares.
- One team owns the schema for the `file` and `folder` namespaces — ownership names who may edit them, nothing stored.

## Contrasts

- `namespace` — the stored, versioned unit of configuration; a schema is only the human grouping over namespaces.
